Top issues to raise with the school

  1. Possible hygiene issue: cups and soup bowls taste of detergent
    A parent reported the cups and soup bowls taste of detergent — a rinsing / dishwashing issue worth checking with the kitchen promptly, since it would also explain some soup being left.
  2. Bones in the fish
    Wednesday's fish was reported to have bones. Worth flagging for prep and safety, especially for the younger children.
  3. Soups are still rejected
    Chicken borsch (1.20), chicken soup (1.57) and tomato soup (1.67) were again the lowest-rated dishes. Same recommendation as last week — rework the recipes, serve them less often, or always offer an alternative the children will eat.
  4. The main can feel small once soup is skipped
    Children who skip the soup find the main course alone too little — one ate two portions of fish to fill up. Consider a slightly larger main, or a reliable second option for soup-skippers.
  5. Keep the served menu matching the published one
    Wednesday's dessert was fruit, not the donuts on the published menu. Small thing, but it affects what families expect.
  6. Only 30 responses this week — encourage more
    Turnout was light (just 1 on Thursday), so these conclusions are tentative. More families filling in the forms would give a clearer picture.

The week in a nutshell

A much quieter week for responses — 30 in total (14 on Monday, then just 4–8 a day, and only 1 on Thursday) — so read this week's numbers as tentative.

Where there is data, it's a better week than the last: overall taste 2.29 / 3 (up from 2.05), 30% of meals rated too cold (down from 38%), and not a single child reported eating 'none' of their lunch. The familiar pattern holds — mains, fried and sweet items do well; soups sit at the bottom.

What kids liked — and what didn't stick

👍 Went down well

  • Breaded cod (2.63) — the Monday standout; one child ate two portions to fill up.
  • The turkey schnitzel / meatballs (3.00) and the chicken-ragu pasta (2.75) — though on small numbers.
  • Donuts (2.80) and the salad option (2.56) — the egg salad was singled out as a favourite.
  • Grilled chicken (2.50) and the potato wedges (2.38).

👎 Didn't stick

  • Soups again — chicken borsch (1.20, the week's worst), chicken soup (1.57) and tomato soup (1.67).
  • The grilled chicken's green herbs put one child off, and the zander fish option scored low (1.67).

A menu-accuracy note (Wednesday's dessert was fruit, not the donuts listed) and a recurring wish for the children to choose their dishes the way the older years do.
