Top issues to raise with the school

  1. Food is reaching children cold
    39% of all meals were rated 'too cold' (and 0% 'too hot'), every day had cold-food comments, and the soups were repeatedly called lukewarm. This is the clearest, most fixable problem — check holding and serving temperatures, especially for soups.
  2. Soups and vegetable stews are consistently rejected
    Every soup / borsch and the vegetarian stews filled the bottom of the rankings all week (chicken soup 1.54 across 39 votes, cabbage 1.40, turkey borsch 1.46, beef 1.75, cauliflower 1.67). Consider reworking the recipes, serving them less often, or always offering an alternative children will eat.
  3. Portions run short and popular dishes run out
    A third of responses said 'too little', and parents repeatedly reported fish and other dishes running out before everyone was served. Review quantities — especially for the dishes kids actually finish (fish, crispy chicken).
  4. Possible food-safety issue on pasta day
    On Tuesday, several parents independently described the cheese as mouldy / blue / sour. This should be checked with the kitchen as a priority, separately from any taste preference.
  5. Children can't see or choose their food
    Repeated comments that kids aren't allowed to look at the options, or to taste a dish unless they commit to eating all of it. A more flexible serving approach could cut both waste and complaints.
  6. Build on what already works
    Lean into the clear winners — crispy chicken strips (2.88, seconds requested), familiar carby sides (rice, potatoes), breaded fish, and fruit / brownie — as the template for dishes children actually finish.

The week in a nutshell

125 families responded across the week (48 on Monday, tapering to 18 by Friday). Overall taste landed at 2.05 / 3 — middling, but polarised rather than uniformly poor: of 529 dish ratings, 199 were 'loved it' and 173 were 'disliked it'.

Two patterns run through every day. First, kids reliably eat familiar, mild, carby / fried / sweet food and reliably reject soups and vegetable stews. Second, and loudest of all, food keeps arriving cold — 39% of meals were rated too cold, and 'everything cold' shows up in the comments every single day.

What kids liked — and what didn't stick

👍 Went down well

  • Crispy chicken strips — the runaway favourite of the week (2.88 / 3), with children asking for seconds.
  • Familiar, starchy sides: simple rice (2.59), oven-baked and mashed potatoes, polenta.
  • Breaded / fried mains and fish — e.g. breadcrumbed cod, sweet & sour chicken, chicken ostropel.
  • Sweet endings: fresh fruit and the brownie cake.

👎 Didn't stick

  • Soups and borsch — every single soup sat in the week's bottom tier (chicken 1.54, turkey borsch 1.46, beef 1.75, cauliflower 1.67), skipped on taste, smell or temperature.
  • Vegetarian vegetable stews: cabbage (1.40), spinach, leek & olives.
  • Anything served cold — the same kinds of dishes were rejected once they arrived lukewarm.
  • Plain mixed / steamed vegetables, especially when kids had to choose them over a dish they preferred.

What families wrote about

The comments converge on a few themes. By far the most frequent is temperature — 'everything cold', 'cold food again', 'always cold' — with the soups singled out as lukewarm.

Quantity and availability come next: portions 'too little', 'not enough', and specific dishes (especially fish) running out before every child is served.

Taste complaints cluster on the soups (watery, no taste, bad smell). One item needs escalating beyond taste: on pasta day (Tuesday) several parents independently described the cheese as mouldy / blue / sour — a possible food-safety issue.

A recurring operational frustration: children say they aren't allowed to look at or choose between dishes, and can't taste something unless they commit to eating it.
